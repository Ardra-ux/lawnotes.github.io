Indian Penal Code, 1860

Overview

The IPC is the principal substantive criminal law in India. It defines crimes, punishments, and principles of criminal liability.

Key Parts

  • General Principles (Ss. 1–52A) → Definitions, punishments, liability.
  • General Exceptions (Ss. 76–106) → Mistake, accident, insanity, intoxication, private defence.
  • Offences against State (Ss. 121–130) → Waging war, sedition.
  • Offences against Human Body (Ss. 299–377) → Murder, culpable homicide, hurt, sexual offences.
  • Offences against Property (Ss. 378–462) → Theft, robbery, dacoity, criminal misappropriation.

Important Sections

  • Sec. 302 – Punishment for Murder
  • Sec. 304 – Culpable Homicide not amounting to Murder
  • Sec. 375 – Definition of Rape
  • Sec. 376 – Punishment for Rape
  • Sec. 498A – Cruelty by Husband or Relatives

Landmark Cases

  • K.M. Nanavati v. State of Maharashtra – Exception of sudden provocation (Sec. 300).
  • Bachan Singh v. State of Punjab – Death penalty constitutional validity (Sec. 302).
