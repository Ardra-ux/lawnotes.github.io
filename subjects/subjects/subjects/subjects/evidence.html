Indian Evidence Act, 1872

Overview

The Indian Evidence Act consolidates the rules of evidence in Indian courts. It defines what facts are relevant and admissible during a trial.

Key Principles

  • Relevancy of Facts → Secs. 5–55.
  • Admissions & Confessions → Secs. 17–31.
  • Statements by Persons who Cannot be Called as Witnesses → Sec. 32 (Dying Declarations).
  • Burden of Proof → Secs. 101–114.
  • Presumptions → Fact presumed unless disproved.

Important Sections

  • Sec. 24 – Confession caused by inducement, threat, or promise is inadmissible.
  • Sec. 32 – Dying Declaration.
  • Sec. 65B – Electronic records admissibility.
  • Sec. 101 – Burden of Proof lies on the party who asserts.

Landmark Cases

  • Pakala Narayana Swami v. Emperor – Dying Declaration scope (Sec. 32).
  • Anvar P.V. v. P.K. Basheer – Electronic evidence & Sec. 65B.
