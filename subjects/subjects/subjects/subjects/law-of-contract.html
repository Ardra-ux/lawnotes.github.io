Law of Contract (Indian Contract Act, 1872)

Overview

The law of contract regulates agreements enforceable by law, creating obligations between parties.

Essential Elements of a Valid Contract

  • Offer and Acceptance (Sec. 2(a), 2(b))
  • Lawful Consideration (Sec. 2(d))
  • Competency of Parties (Sec. 11)
  • Free Consent (Sec. 14)
  • Lawful Object

Important Contracts

  • Void & Voidable Contracts
  • Quasi-Contracts (Sec. 68–72)
  • Contingent Contracts (Sec. 31–36)

Landmark Cases

  • Carlill v. Carbolic Smoke Ball Co. – Unilateral contract.
  • Mohori Bibee v. Dharmodas Ghose – Minor’s agreement void.
