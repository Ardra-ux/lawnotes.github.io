Criminal Procedure Code, 1973

Overview

The CrPC is the procedural law for criminal trials in India. It provides the mechanism for investigation, inquiry, trial, and punishment of offences under the IPC and other laws.

Key Features

  • Investigation → Police powers, FIR (Sec. 154), arrest, search and seizure.
  • Bail & Remand → Provisions for bail, custody of accused.
  • Trial Procedures → Different types: Sessions trial, warrant case, summons case, summary trial.
  • Appeals, Revisions, References → Higher court remedies.
  • Maintenance → Sec. 125 – Maintenance for wife, children, parents.

Important Sections

  • Sec. 154 – First Information Report (FIR)
  • Sec. 167 – Remand to police/judicial custody
  • Sec. 197 – Sanction to prosecute public servants
  • Sec. 320 – Compounding of offences
  • Sec. 438 – Anticipatory Bail

Landmark Cases

  • Lalita Kumari v. Govt. of U.P. – FIR registration is mandatory under Sec. 154.
  • State of Haryana v. Bhajan Lal – Guidelines for quashing FIR under Sec. 482 CrPC.
